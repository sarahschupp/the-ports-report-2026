Accessibility

Effective April 26, 2026

Our Commitment

NAI Robert Lynn is committed to ensuring The Ports Report is accessible to users of all abilities. We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, as published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C).

Measures We Have Taken

  • Semantic HTML structure with appropriate heading hierarchy
  • All interactive elements are keyboard navigable
  • Color contrast ratios meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA standards (verified: NAI Red on white = 5.9:1; charcoal on white = 16.3:1)
  • All images include descriptive alt text or are marked decorative with aria-hidden="true"
  • SVG logos include aria-label attributes
  • Focus indicators are visible on all interactive elements
  • The report is operable without a mouse using keyboard-only navigation

Known Limitations

The interactive U.S. port map uses SVG with mouse-based hover states. Screen reader users can access all underlying port data through the Port Briefs and Decision Matrix sections, which are fully accessible via keyboard. We are working to improve the map's accessibility in a future update.

Assistive Technology

The Ports Report has been tested with VoiceOver (macOS/iOS) and is designed to be compatible with NVDA, JAWS, and TalkBack. If you experience difficulty accessing content with your assistive technology, please contact us and we will provide the information in an alternative format.

Feedback and Requests

We welcome feedback on accessibility barriers. If you encounter a problem accessing any section of this report, please contact us. We aim to respond within 2 business days and to resolve substantive issues within 10 business days.
