Strategic Implication

Houston is structurally different from every other gateway on this list: it is one of the only major U.S. ports where exports exceed imports. This inverts the typical industrial real estate thesis. FTZ #84 inverted tariff benefits are unmatched in the Gulf region — components enter duty-free and finished goods pay the lower finished-product rate. For manufacturers importing machinery and raw materials into the U.S. Gulf corridor, the total landed cost differential vs. coastal peers is 12–18%. Model this before signing anywhere else.

Strategic Implication

The Mason Mega Rail Terminal's 22-hour vessel-to-rail transfer is now the operational benchmark every competing East Coast port is chasing. Savannah is planning a 60% capacity expansion — but the build-to-suit incentive stack in Pooler and Statesboro is tightening in lockstep with that commitment. Clients who require Southeast distribution into the I-75/I-95 corridor should close site selection before Q4 2026 or face a materially different cost structure.

Strategic Implication

Long Beach's 9.9M TEU record was built on frontloading ahead of anticipated tariff escalation — a demand pattern that is now working through retailer inventory. Vacancy in the Inland Empire has eased from its 2024 low of 3.4% to 4.2% as new industrial supply absorbs. This is a brief window. West Coast labor volatility and drayage cost structure ($850–$1,100 per container) remain structural headwinds for firms modeling inbound vs. East Coast alternatives.
